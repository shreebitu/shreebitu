FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? Here are the most common things users ask about virtual Android apps and custom ROMs.

Are the APKs on ModFire safe to download?+
Yes. Every APK listed on ModFire.in is scanned for malware using multiple antivirus engines before being published. We also provide SHA256 checksums so you can verify file integrity. However, we always recommend downloading from official sources first, and using our listings as supplementary guides.
Do virtual Android apps work on all devices?+
Virtual Android apps generally require Android 5.0 or higher. Performance is best on devices with 4GB+ RAM and a modern processor. Some apps like VMOS Pro use virtualization extensions that may not be available on all chipsets. Always check the compatibility requirements on each app's page before downloading.
Do I need to root my device to use virtual Android apps?+
No â€” most virtual Android apps like VMOS Pro, Twoyi, and VirtualXposed work without root on your main device. The virtual environment itself can have root access enabled independently. This is one of the biggest advantages: root features inside the VM without compromising your real device's security.
How do I install a Custom ROM?+
Installing a custom ROM requires: (1) unlocking your device's bootloader, (2) installing a custom recovery like TWRP, (3) backing up your data, and (4) flashing the ROM zip through recovery. Each ROM page on ModFire includes device-specific installation guides. Be sure to match the ROM exactly to your device model number.
Will installing a custom ROM void my warranty?+
Typically yes â€” unlocking the bootloader and flashing custom software voids the manufacturer warranty on most devices. However, some devices and regions have exceptions. Custom ROMs also typically cannot be tracked by manufacturers after flashing. Always research your specific device and regional warranty policies before proceeding.
Can I run virtual Android apps on iOS?+
No â€” virtual Android apps are exclusively for Android devices. iOS has a completely different architecture and app ecosystem that does not support Android virtualization. For iOS users looking to run Android apps, the only alternative is using Android Studio emulator on a desktop computer.
Do virtual Android apps affect device performance?+
Running a virtual Android environment does consume additional RAM and CPU resources. On mid-to-high-end devices, the impact is minimal. On devices with less than 3GB RAM, you may experience slowdowns. Apps like VMOS Lite are specifically optimized for low-end devices to minimize performance impact.
